About

My name is Joud Baddawi. I am a 16-year-old girl living in Amman, Jordan.
In the future, I hope to become a software developer. I want to attend university and own a smart car - preferably red!

I joined Code Chica to learn the basics of building and designing websites. I want to create my own portfolio and expand from there to many, many more websites.
I love to listen to music while studying, and I enjoy watching my favorite movies on my laptop. I use technology to have fun or connect with friends.


I live with my family. My mom and dad, my two brothers, and my sister.
I am a junior at Al Jazeera School, and my favorite classes are English and Chemistry. Outside of school, I enjoy coding and reading. I am more of an indoor person, but I play basketball and skate to stay active!

This is my personal website. Welcome!

Reading

Reading is one of my favorite hobbies. I enjoy all kinds of books, but the genres I love the most are historical fiction and astronomy books! I love reading poetry as well. My favorite authors are Ayman Ottom, Ahmad Kayre Al Omari, and Guillaume Musso. My favorite poets are Al mutanabi and Kaleel Jubran.



Creative Writing

Creative writing is a result of reading all the time! Although I don't count creative writing as a fun way to spend time, I love participating in school and nationwide competitions. In 2020 I participated in my latest national competition and won 100 JOD! (~140 USD). I love to write Arabic literature rather than English literature because the more comprehensive Arabic vocabulary allows for more artistic and creative writing. However, the English grammar rules are more forgiving.



Coding

Coding is a passion of mine. So far, I've mastered C++, Python, and HTML. The most painless language to learn for me was HTML. The most challenging is JavaScript. I'm still learning JS, and CodeChica helped me in that aspect. Every coding course I participated in left me with lifelong friends! Maybe that's why I'm so passionate about it. What I love the most about coding is the happiness you feel when you FINALLY catch that bug that was ruining your program.
